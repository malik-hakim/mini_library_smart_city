Smart Environment

Smart Environment is defined as a small world where various intelligent devices work continuously to improve the living comfort of its residents. The main goal is to create an adaptive, efficient and safe environment by utilizing technology to replace dangerous work, heavy physical labor and repetitive tasks. Thus, smart environments focus on comfort, safety, efficiency and sustainability.

Success Indicators

Smart environment has a number of success indicators that play an important role in creating a smart city that is both environmentally friendly and sustainable. Efficient water management and integrated waste systems are the main basis for maintaining the sustainability of natural resources, so that people's needs can be met without sacrificing future generations. Utilization of renewable energy is also a key factor in supporting the transition to clean energy, reducing dependence on fossil energy, and reducing negative impacts on the environment.

Apart from that, the success of a smart environment is also determined by participatory governance that involves the community in decision making, so that the policies made are more targeted and sustainable. Collaboration between stakeholders strengthens synergy in implementing environmental policies, while technological innovation enables early detection, prediction and more effective resolution of environmental problems. With a combination of these indicators, a smart environment not only supports modern urban development, but also creates a balance between human needs and environmental sustainability.
